MASTER SERVICES AGREEMENT

This Master Services Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of January 15, 2025
("Effective Date"), by and between:

BUYER: Acme Corp, a Delaware corporation with its principal office at 100 Innovation Drive,
San Francisco, CA 94105 ("Customer")

VENDOR: CloudServe Solutions Inc., a Delaware corporation with its principal office at 2500
Technology Boulevard, Austin, TX 78701 ("Provider")

RECITALS
WHEREAS, Provider offers cloud-based enterprise software solutions and related services;
WHEREAS, Customer desires to engage Provider to provide such services subject to the terms
and conditions set forth herein.

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements set forth herein,
the parties agree as follows:

1. DEFINITIONS
1.1 "Services" means the cloud-based project management and collaboration platform
    described in Exhibit A, including all updates and maintenance.
1.2 "Customer Data" means all data submitted by Customer through the Services.
1.3 "Derivative Works" means any modifications, enhancements, customizations, or
    integrations developed in connection with or based upon the Services.
1.4 "Confidential Information" means any non-public information disclosed by either party.

2. SCOPE OF SERVICES
2.1 Provider shall make the Services available to Customer on a software-as-a-service basis.
2.2 Provider shall provide up to 500 user licenses as part of this Agreement.
2.3 Provider shall provide standard email support during business hours (9 AM - 5 PM CT),
    Monday through Friday, excluding federal holidays.
2.4 Premium support (24/7 phone and chat) is available for an additional fee of $3,000/month.

3. FEES AND PAYMENT
3.1 Customer shall pay Provider a monthly fee of $15,000 ("Monthly Fee"), due within
    thirty (30) days of invoice date.
3.2 Provider may increase the Monthly Fee upon thirty (30) days' written notice to Customer.
    Customer's continued use of the Services after such notice constitutes acceptance of the
    new fee. [NOTE: Industry standard is typically 60-90 days notice with a cap on increases]
3.3 Late payments shall accrue interest at a rate of 1.5% per month.
3.4 All fees are exclusive of applicable taxes.

4. TERM AND RENEWAL
4.1 The initial term of this Agreement shall be three (3) years from the Effective Date
    ("Initial Term").
4.2 This Agreement shall automatically renew for successive one (1) year periods
    ("Renewal Terms") unless either party provides written notice of non-renewal at least
    ninety (90) days prior to the end of the then-current term.
    [NOTE: 90-day notice period is unusually long; 30-60 days is standard]
4.3 Early termination by Customer during the Initial Term shall require payment of all
    remaining Monthly Fees for the balance of the Initial Term.

5. SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENT
5.1 Provider guarantees 99.5% uptime for the Services, measured on a monthly basis,
    excluding scheduled maintenance windows.
5.2 Scheduled maintenance shall occur between 2:00 AM and 6:00 AM CT on Sundays.
5.3 Service credits for downtime exceeding the SLA:
    - 99.0% - 99.5%: 5% credit on Monthly Fee
    - 98.0% - 99.0%: 10% credit on Monthly Fee
    - Below 98.0%: 20% credit on Monthly Fee
5.4 Service credits are Customer's sole and exclusive remedy for any failure to meet the SLA.
5.5 Customer must request service credits within fifteen (15) days of the incident.

6. DATA PROTECTION AND SECURITY
6.1 Provider shall implement commercially reasonable security measures to protect
    Customer Data, including encryption at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.2+).
6.2 Provider shall comply with SOC 2 Type II requirements.
6.3 Customer Data shall be stored in the United States.
6.4 Upon termination, Provider shall delete Customer Data within ninety (90) days unless
    otherwise required by law. Customer may request an export of Customer Data within
    thirty (30) days of termination.
6.5 Provider may use anonymized, aggregated Customer Data for product improvement,
    benchmarking, and marketing purposes.

7. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY
7.1 Provider retains all rights, title, and interest in and to the Services, including all
    intellectual property rights therein.
7.2 All Derivative Works, including but not limited to custom integrations, plugins,
    workflows, templates, and configurations developed by Customer or jointly by the
    parties, shall be the sole property of Provider.
    [NOTE: This is highly unusual. Custom integrations built by the buyer typically remain
    the buyer's IP, or at minimum are jointly owned.]
7.3 Customer retains all rights to Customer Data.
7.4 Provider grants Customer a non-exclusive, non-transferable license to use the Services
    during the term of this Agreement.

8. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY
8.1 IN NO EVENT SHALL EITHER PARTY'S TOTAL AGGREGATE LIABILITY UNDER THIS
    AGREEMENT EXCEED THE FEES PAID BY CUSTOMER DURING THE ONE (1) MONTH
    PERIOD IMMEDIATELY PRECEDING THE EVENT GIVING RISE TO THE CLAIM.
    [NOTE: Liability cap of 1 month's fees ($15,000) is extremely low for a 3-year,
    $540,000 contract. Industry standard is typically 12 months of fees.]
8.2 NEITHER PARTY SHALL BE LIABLE FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL,
    CONSEQUENTIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES.
8.3 The limitations in this Section 8 shall not apply to (a) breaches of Section 6
    (Data Protection), (b) willful misconduct, or (c) indemnification obligations.

9. INDEMNIFICATION
9.1 Provider shall indemnify Customer against third-party claims alleging that the Services
    infringe any intellectual property rights, provided Customer promptly notifies Provider
    and provides reasonable cooperation.
9.2 Customer shall indemnify Provider against third-party claims arising from Customer's
    use of the Services in violation of this Agreement or applicable law.

10. TERMINATION
10.1 Either party may terminate this Agreement for cause if the other party materially
     breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days of
     written notice.
10.2 Provider may suspend Services immediately upon notice if Customer fails to pay any
     undisputed fees within sixty (60) days of the due date.
10.3 Upon termination for any reason, Customer shall pay all fees accrued through the
     effective date of termination.

11. CONFIDENTIALITY
11.1 Each party agrees to maintain the confidentiality of the other party's Confidential
     Information for a period of five (5) years following disclosure.
11.2 Confidential Information may be disclosed to employees and contractors with a
     need to know, subject to obligations of confidentiality.

12. GENERAL PROVISIONS
12.1 Governing Law: This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of Texas.
12.2 Dispute Resolution: Any dispute shall first be subject to good-faith negotiation for
     thirty (30) days, followed by binding arbitration in Austin, Texas.
12.3 Assignment: Neither party may assign this Agreement without the other's prior written
     consent, except in connection with a merger or acquisition.
12.4 Force Majeure: Neither party shall be liable for delays caused by events beyond its
     reasonable control.
12.5 Entire Agreement: This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties.
12.6 Amendments: This Agreement may only be modified in writing signed by both parties.
12.7 Notices: All notices shall be in writing and delivered to the addresses set forth above.
